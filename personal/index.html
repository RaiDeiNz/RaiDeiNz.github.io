WHO IS RAI?

Hello World! Not as a programmer; but as a newborn in this story.

On a chilly winter day in 1995, as the night cast its dark claw over Samsun, at 21:05 I took my first breath and opened my eyes to the world. Born with bloodied hands and I arrived in this world ready to fight with my little fists. Little did my mother know that she was about to endure the most challenging birth of her life. While countless mothers left the hospital with their newborns, my mother remained under the watchful eye of the medical staff. Weeks went by, and still, she stayed. From the moment I was born, it seemed as though I had imposed upon my family the responsibility to fight alongside me. Perhaps, that night, the fabric of my destiny was woven into my very being. Samsun, the city of my birth, was a place where struggles took root, where battles were conceived. It was here that the seeds of an unwavering spirit were planted within me, destined to guide me through a lifetime of challenges. Who knows, maybe that very day marked the beginning of my insatiable thirst for knowledge. Today, as I sense the world's ailing state, I am filled with a profound sense of duty. I am compelled to seek solutions, not just for my nation, but for our neighboring civilizations and the entire world. Like my esteemed ancestor, Bilge Kagan, I aspire to unite my people and heal the world. This is the story of my life, from the day of my birth to the present moment, a tale of resilience, purpose, and the relentless pursuit of a better future...

Before I existed


Let me share with you the story from a time preceding my birth. According to the ancient Turkic belief, the skills of a Kam (a type of shaman) were passed down from parent to child. Keep this information in mind as it will play a role in my future life story.

On my maternal side, I am descended from the Kagan and the even more ancient Scythian Turks. The region around the Caspian Sea has been the ancestral home of Turkish tribes throughout history. Although not documented in official history, this knowledge has been passed down through family sources. The Khan of the Chepni Tribe "Haci Emiroglu principality..."

Throughout history, conflicts persisted among my relatives over the division of land and wealth. From the titled lands spanning from Samsun to Georgia; to treasures such as the Tsarina's necklace, these disputes were fueled by greed. It's worth noting that this necklace was acquired through legitimate means, not raided. (I mention this to avoid any misinterpretation as we incorporate Russia into our story). These conflicts were sanctioned by a fatwa, issued by self-proclaimed scholars(ulema) of a false religion(like other all) steeped in ignorance. The fatwa proclaimed, "The children of the Khanate can kill each other, preventing the people from dividing and killing one another." Thus, similar to other Arab religions (Judaism, Christianity, and Islam), a new narrative was created to reduce the Turkish population.

These distressing memories marked the beginning of a challenging journey within my mother's lineage. It was a journey characterized by constant relocation from one city to another and from one country to another. Our surname changed numerous times, and my grandmother grew weary. She prayed to Tengri, the supreme deity of our ancestral belief, to grant her four children: two daughters and two sons. Tengri answered her prayers, and indeed, she had four children, with my mother being the youngest among them. As part of the lineage, in the history, the prefix "Haji" was adopted, reflecting the influence of the Islamic religion. (When we conquered Anatolia, a different ulema was assigned to each Seljuk principality, and they taught the religion of Islam to facilitate interaction with the surrounding culture. This is where Haji -Haci in Turkish- prefix comes in.) However, my grandmother wished to pass away before she could undertake the pilgrimage that would grant her the title of Haji. Surprisingly, her wish was granted by Tengri, as she died during the journey. While everyone else in the vehicle survived, the accident claimed my grandmother's life. When my grandmother passed away, my mother was just over 10 years old. But her story truly begins with my birth...

0~14 Age Period


Before my mother got married, she dreamed of a group of soldiers: One of them left the group as they were going up the slope and came back. The soldier who left the group married my mother. My mother wanted one child all her life.

We are three siblings. They were surprise births for my mother. I became the only child my mother ever conceived by wanting to have a child. My father is a former commander. When his military unit was battered by his superior, he became disobedient to his superior and beat him. According to my father, no soldier should be ridiculed. If even God descended to earth and disrespected his soldiers, he would declare war on God. In my father's inner world, the soldier is sacred. As a result, my father left his military service. When my mother married my father, she had not yet revealed her dream to him, because she did not know about this past of my father. My mother knew my father as an insurer. When he met my mother, my father was a wealthy and tall insurer. A nomadic lineage that is said to be descended from Hacı Fettahoğulları. They migrated through Trabzon. My paternal great-grandfather was an Ottoman captain who provided supplies to the Ahiska Turks. When the Russians came to seize his ship, he rescued his crew and abandoned the ship. When my great-grandfather brought the crew ashore, he jumped into the water and swam for miles at sea. From one bay to another. From one county to another. From one village to another. He climbed the mountain from the last bay he reached and made it a village. Over the years, my grandfather had neighbors. Then he had a son, a teacher named Yaman. And then my grandfather Yaman brought my grandmother Meral to Samsun to the hospital and then she gave birth to my father. Somehow, my father's and my mother's fates are tied together. Both were born in Samsun and I was born in Samsun. Unlike my siblings. The year I was born, fate turns back. Nomadism...

From Samsun to Ordu, from Ordu to Merzifon, from Merzifon to Ünye, from Ünye to Hakkari, from Hakkari to İzmir, from İzmir to Denizli, from Denizli to Samsun, then From Samsun to Alanya, and finally from Alanya back to Samsun. Maybe there are even some I forgot... Exactly...

I learned writing and math until I was 5 years old. At the age of 5, my mother enrolled me in a boarding nursery. I was going to a painting course during the remaining time from boarding nursery. I started kindergarten at the age of 6. This time, I was taking ballet training in the remaining time. When I started the first year of primary school at the age of 7, conservatory (choir) and theater were added to my ballet training. I had a fast pace. Still, this was not a problem for my mother, because I was a child diagnosed with hyperactivity. When we were going to go to the stage performance and crown the training and receive an award as applause from the audience, I was ashamed to go on stage because I couldn't do a single move. Even though I didn't explain the situation to my Yugoslavian teacher, I think he would have understood the problem so he planned the performance show without that single move, and he gave me one last lesson that I will never forget for the rest of my life. "My failures and shame shouldn't stop me from taking action!"

My mother was a civil servant who taught high school and middle school students in three languages. My mother, who had been proud of me as I gained success after success in Elementary School, was worn out by all of our relocation and seemed like she couldn't take care of me any longer. Just for this reason, he decided to retire while we were moving to Denizli. However, increasingly disinterested behavior in all these years started a period of disinterest in education in me. My grades were constantly declining and I was no longer interested in any hobby other than skating. By the time I reached the final year of secondary school and took the exam to move on to high school, I had completely lost my focus.
